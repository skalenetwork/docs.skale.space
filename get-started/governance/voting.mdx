## Overview

SKL token holders can vote on governance proposals to influence network decisions. Voting is a key way to participate in SKALE Network governance.

## Voting Process

The voting process includes:

1. **Review Proposals** - Review active governance proposals
2. **Understand Impact** - Understand the impact of each proposal
3. **Cast Vote** - Cast your vote using your delegated SKL tokens
4. **Monitor Results** - Monitor voting results and proposal execution

## Voting Power Calculation

Your voting power is based on:

- **Delegated SKL** - Amount of SKL tokens you have delegated
- **Staking Status** - Your tokens must be actively staked
- **Validator Selection** - The validator you've delegated to

## How to Vote

To vote on governance proposals:

1. **Ensure Delegation** - Ensure your SKL tokens are delegated
2. **Access Governance Portal** - Access the SKALE governance portal
3. **Review Active Proposals** - Review proposals currently open for voting
4. **Cast Your Vote** - Cast your vote (For/Against/Abstain)
5. **Confirm Transaction** - Confirm the voting transaction

## Voting Requirements

To vote on proposals, you must:

- **Hold SKL Tokens** - Have SKL tokens in your wallet
- **Delegate Tokens** - Delegate your SKL tokens to a validator
- **Active Staking** - Have your tokens actively staked
- **Network Connection** - Be connected to Ethereum Mainnet

## Best Practices

- **Research Proposals** - Thoroughly research proposals before voting
- **Consider Impact** - Consider the long-term impact on the network
- **Participate Actively** - Actively participate in governance discussions
- **Stay Informed** - Stay informed about network developments
